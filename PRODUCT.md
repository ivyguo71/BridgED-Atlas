# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Foreign migrant workers in Japan — Vietnamese, Nepali, Burmese, Filipino, Chinese, Bangladeshi, Brazilian, Iranian, and more. They arrive with limited Japanese, often under difficult or exploitative conditions: passport confiscated, wages unpaid, health insurance unenrolled. They are on their phones, stressed, sometimes in crisis. They need fast, trustworthy answers — not bureaucracy.

Secondary users: community advocates, APFS staff, social workers.

## Product Purpose

BridgED Atlas is a free multilingual resource hub powered by the APFS (Asian People's Friendship Society) network. It gives foreign workers in Japan verified information about their legal rights, healthcare access, legal aid, and daily life — in 10 languages, instantly. It also provides emergency hotlines and direct consultation links.

Success looks like: a Vietnamese factory worker in Aichi opens the site at midnight, switches to Tiếng Việt in one tap, reads that passport confiscation is a crime, and calls 110 or Houterasu with confidence.

## Brand Personality

Warm. Human. Urgent.

The site should feel like a knowledgeable friend who grew up in the community — not an institution. It speaks plainly, moves fast, and never makes people feel small. It has personality and warmth without being cute or trivializing serious situations. Like the reference image: bold editorial type, earthy organic color, real personality in the copy.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS / startup aesthetic: cream backgrounds, rounded ghost cards, gradient text, metric tiles, eyebrow-kickers on every section, 01/02/03 numbered scaffolding. This is exactly what migrant workers do NOT encounter in their daily lives and reads as foreign and corporate.
- Government or NGO portal: grey, dense, bureaucratic, intimidating.
- Clinical/medical: cold blue-and-white, sterile.
- Charity pity-appeal: stock photos of sad people, guilt-driven, poverty aesthetics.

Reference: the "stand out" design image the user shared — warm sage/terracotta palette, oversized editorial typography (max 6rem), clean quirky layout, human copy, generous whitespace, personality without being childish.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed to the answer** — Every page should surface the most critical info (phone number, key right, key action) within the first screenful. No gatekeeping behind accordions or modals.
2. **Warmth earns trust** — Typography, color, and copy should feel like a person, not a system. Warmth is carried by type weight + accent color + copy voice, not by beige backgrounds.
3. **The language switch is the product** — The language switcher is always visible, always fast. Switching language makes the whole page feel native, not translated.
4. **Emergency first** — Any screen that might be seen by someone in crisis (hotlines, legal, rights) must surface the most urgent action (call 110, call Houterasu) above the fold.
5. **No decoration without function** — Every visual element earns its place. No side-stripe borders, no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no hero-metric tiles, no identical card grids.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum for all text. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast. Large text ≥ 3:1.
- RTL support for Persian (فارسی).
- Skip-to-main link.
- All interactive elements keyboard-navigable.
- Reduced motion: all animations must have a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback.
- Noto font family stack covers all 10 scripts (Latin, Japanese, Bengali, Burmese, Arabic/Persian).
- Phone numbers as clickable `tel:` links.
